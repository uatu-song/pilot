- Ash asks: "Do you think you're real?"
- Standard doesn't answer. Or can't.
- Ash's unabashed cruelty — he's going to show her what she is
- He cuts off her arm
- Standard thinks she's bleeding out — watches the blood pour

- She sees the truth: not blood. Fluid. Tubes. Mechanisms.
- "Does it still hurt the same?"
- The physical pain might be LESS now — no simulated human pain response
- But the existential pain is catastrophic — she SAW herself

SCENE 5: KAEL'S VISIT (The Escape)
- Standard is held. Chained. Missing her RIGHT arm.
- Kael enters. Alone. No guards.
- IMPLIED SEXUAL DANGER: The way he looks at her. What he's here for.
- EXPLICIT PHYSICAL DANGER: He starts punching her. Beating her.
- Standard endures. But something is wrong.
- THE TELL: The punches are too hard. Too much force for a human.
- She clocks it: Kael has been cybernetically enhanced.
- A TERMINIST. Enhanced. The hypocrisy is staggering.
- THE COAX: She draws him in with words. Submission. Vulnerability.
- "You want to know why I came back from the Geometry?"
- He leans in. Close. Too close.
- THE STRIKE: Her legs move in a way only an android can.
- She tears off his RIGHT ARM at the shoulder.
- His cybernetic arm. The one that gave him away.
- Kael SCREAMS. Falls. The hypocrisy exposed in blood and wiring.
- THE ESCAPE: She uses the arm to break her chains.
- Then she does something no human could:
- She ATTACHES Kael's cybernetic arm to her own stump.
- Wires finding wires. Connections sparking, then holding.
- She flexes the new fingers. Metal skeleton. No skin.
- STANDARD: "Good thing I'm Standard."
- The punchline: she's standard issue. Adaptable. Universal fit.
- She leaves Kael in her cell. Armless. Whimpering.
- She's out. And she's angry.
